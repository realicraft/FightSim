This page details things that occur in Fighting Simulator 3.

Formatting

FS3 uses various types of formatting to signify different things.
This section will show what the various types of formatting mean.
Bold text signifies an effect.
Italic text signifies something that is on the island that everyone fights on.
Underlined text signifies something that could be added by voting on it.

Types

There are 8 types: [color=gray]Normal[/color], [color=orange]Fire[/color], [color=blue]Water[/color], [color=yellow]Electric[/color], [color=green]Plant[/color], [color=cyan]Ice[/color], [color=lightgray]Light[/color], and [color=darkgray]Dark[/color].
The type effectiveness works like this:
[code]
               Dark ->  Normal  -> Light              
    Water, Electric ->   Fire   -> Plant, Ice         
         Plant, Ice ->  Water   -> Fire               
               Dark -> Electric -> Fire, Dark         
               Fire ->  Plant   -> Water, Light       
               Fire ->   Ice    -> Water              
Dark, Plant, Normal ->  Light   -> Dark               
    Light, Electric ->   Dark   -> Normal, Electric   
[/code]
Additionally, some effects behave differently with certain types. Specifically:
[quote]On Fire: Fire types are healed rather than harmed, Ice and Plant types take +2 damage per turn, Water types take -1 damage per turn and have a 20% chance per turn to have the effect tick down an extra turn.
Frozen: Ice types gain a +3 defense boost rather than the standard effects, and Water types have a 20% chance per turn for the effect to not tick down.
Paralysis: Electric types gain a +3 attack boost rather than the standard effects, and Dark types gain a -2 attack loss.
Poisoned: Dark types are immune, and Light types always take damage.[/quote]
You can change type at any time, but it takes effect at the end of the turn.
