Formatting

FS3 uses various types of formatting to signify different things.
This section will show what the various types of formatting mean.
Bold text signifies an effect.
Italic text signifies something that is on the island that everyone fights on.
Underlined text signifies something that could be added by voting on it.

Skill bonuses

Reaching levels 5 and 10 of a skill will grants bonuses based on the skill.

Skill Level 5 Bonus Level 10 Bonus
Farming Critical Misses no longer destroy the crop, and gain 1 extra crop on Critical Hits. Once per post, you can pick one crop, owned by anyone, to give an extra turn of growth to.
Mining When mining for nothing in particular, gain 1 extra ore of any type. +2 Direct Defense.
Chopping Non-Chopping skill rolls now have a 40% chance to give you one Stick. When attacking, ignore 30% of the enemy's Direct Defense.
Crafting Crafted items gain a 25% boost to an bonuses they have. For every item consumed by crafting, there's a 25% chance to not consume the item. This does not apply to thin-air items.
Carving You no longer need to use a skill roll to sharpen your weapon. +2 Direct Attack.
Cooking You suffer 33% less of the effects from food-born debuffs, and heal an additional 2 HP from crops. Once per turn, you can pick one person to heal by 20 HP and give Regen 2 to.
Construction +2 Indirect Defense, and Critical Misses no longer damage what's being worked on. Once per post, you can pick one person or structure to create a wall around without needing to use a skill roll.
Gathering When finding items from any non-Gathering skill roll, you gain 15% more items. Once per post, you can pick one item source to gain an item from without needing to use a skill roll, and without resetting any counters.
Digging Skill rolls that affect the environment (such as Mining or Chopping) have a 50% chance to give you 1 Dirt, Sand, or Gravel. You can now throw blocks like Sand or Gravel at someone to Blind them. This deals 50% more damage than it would otherwise.
Gardening Critical Misses no longer destroy the plant, and gain 1 extra plant on Critical Hits. You can eat one plant that wouldn't normally give an effect per post to gain a status effect based on the plant. Plants that normally give effects on consumption are unaffected by the limit.

Weather

Occasionally, the weather will change, resulting in various effects.

Things that rely on the sun will have reduced effectiveness when it's not Day. This does not apply to plants, and both Night and Partly Cloudy reduce effectiveness less than other weather types.

Type Effect
Day No special effects.
Night No special effects.
Rain Crops grow slightly faster, fires are extinguished quicker, and items and structures might get soaked.
Downpour Like rain, but worse. Crops and Structures have a chance to get buffeted by the rain, damaging them, and exposed fires are put out immediately. Additionally, Structures can be Flooded.
Cloudy No special effects.
Partly Cloudy No special effects.
Thunderstorm Similar to rain, with the added effect of structures occasionally getting hit by lightning.
Lightning Similar to a thunderstorm, but without the rain.
Snow It's colder, crops grow slower, and structures might get covered in snow.
